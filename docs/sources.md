# Candidate influence sources (Australia)

This is an initial seed list of organisations likely to influence parliamentary discussion, committee submissions, and bill design. Sources were selected from public websites and can be expanded as ingestion improves.

## Think tanks and policy institutes

- **Grattan Institute** — independent policy research; publishes reports across energy, housing, health, tax, democracy.
  - https://grattan.edu.au
- **Lowy Institute** — international policy and security research; relevant for foreign policy, defence, Pacific, trade.
  - https://www.lowyinstitute.org
- **The Australia Institute** — public policy think tank with active reports/submissions (economics, climate, democracy).
  - https://australiainstitute.org.au
- **Centre for Independent Studies (CIS)** — policy papers/opinion across economics, education, energy, foreign policy.
  - https://www.cis.org.au
- **Institute of Public Affairs (IPA)** — policy research and parliamentary briefs across legal rights, economy, energy, security.
  - https://ipa.org.au
- **Per Capita** — progressive think tank with submissions and policy work (work, housing, education, technology).
  - https://percapita.org.au

## Peak bodies and advocacy/lobby organisations

- **Business Council of Australia (BCA)** — major business peak body; publishes submissions, media releases, policy agendas.
  - https://www.bca.com.au
- **Australian Council of Social Service (ACOSS)** — social policy peak body; campaigns and media statements on welfare/tax/housing.
  - https://www.acoss.org.au

## Academic and evidence sources with policy influence

- **Australian National University (ANU)** — major policy-relevant research output and polling.
  - https://www.anu.edu.au
- **Productivity Commission** — independent government research/advice with direct policy relevance to bills and reforms.
  - https://www.pc.gov.au
- **Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS)** — core national statistics used in policy debate and explanatory memoranda.
  - https://www.abs.gov.au

## Parliamentary channels where influence is visible

- **Parliamentary Committees portal** — public submissions/hearings provide direct evidence of stakeholder influence.
  - https://www.aph.gov.au/Parliamentary_Business/Committees

## Notes for extraction design

- Prioritise harvesting: `reports`, `submissions`, `media releases`, `committee evidence`, `transcripts`.
- Record metadata for each item: organisation, date, URL, document type, topic tags, quoted bill/act references.
- Match claims against bill timelines using committee inquiry dates, bill introduction/amendment dates, and Hansard mentions.
